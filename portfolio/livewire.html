63 days to design and build a product launch experience for the world’s largest consumer trade show.

In 2019 H-D was ready to unveil their latest creation. The fastest, most eco-friendly, and quietest bike they’ve ever made.
Not exactly features the iconic 115-year-old brand is known for.

So how do you unveil an all-electric Harley that absolutely no one is expecting?

You take the brand somewhere they’ve never been, introduce them to an entirely new audience, and watch people enjoy the thrill of riding a Harley.

This was a once-in-a-lifetime creative opportunity, albeit one that came with its own set of limitations and constraints. We had to build an experience that would steal an unfair share of attention at the world's largest consumer trade show, without stealing attention from the bike itself. We had very limited space on the showroom floor, and essentially zero access to the single working prototype bike in existence. And we had 63 calendar days to design, build, test and deliver.

What emerged was a Magic Leap-based mixed-reality experience that enabled riders to feel the precise speed and acceleration of the newest Harley-Davidson motorcycle.

Participants were able to sit on the first-ever all electric Livewire motorcycle and get a feel for the dimensions, trim, and cutting edge engineering that H-D is known for. Then when it was time to take it for a test drive all they had to do was fire it up and punch the throttle. Many were skeptical at first as the fully functional bike would sprint to life and our augmented world would fly around them in 3-dimensional space. Acceleration and speed data was pulled live from sensors integrated beneath the rear tire rollers on the dyno. Allowing even the most novice riders to feel the true 0-60 speed and seamlessly control the entire experience from the throttle itself.

Ultimately, the experience was such a success at CES, we worked with our tremendous production partners at Red Paper Heart to build multiple versions that will tour sponsored events worldwide throughout 2019 and beyond.
